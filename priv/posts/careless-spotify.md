## 🫶 Spotify

First off, let me get this off my chest. Spotify, use more of your revenue to pay the artists that provide you the platform.

Since 2018, I have been using Spotify to discover new music and build my playlists. One playlist per month to hold all the music I discovered that month. I have always appreciated the platform's ability to help me discover new music.

It's a blast to be able to go back, pick any year and any month and listen to the songs I discovered that month. So, like any nerd would do, I built a Spotify app to help automate that.

## Spotify could not care less

I want to share my music discoveries and see what others have discovered but Spotify has made it much harder to do that. And it seems like they don't care.

It seems they now are treating developers like a nuisance instead of an integral part of their community. Spotify's priorities seem to be money, control, then the community. The artists, users, and developers come after the money.

On February 6 of this year (2026), Spotify announced that the following changes to their 'Developer Access' program:

1. Reducing the number of users allowed in 'Development Mode' from 25 to 5.
2. Developers are required to be on any paid plan to access the Spotify Web API.
3. API access to various endpoints have been removed.
4. Developers can only have one app per account in 'Development Mode'.
5. To move out of developer mode, developers must prove a monthly minimum of 250k active users. *Introduced May 15, 2025*

This is a huge blow to developers who are trying to build apps that expand Spotify's platform's reach.
